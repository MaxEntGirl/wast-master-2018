Title: Von Ludwig Wittgenstein an Gilbert Pattisson, [vor 15. 4. 1933]

sourceDesc:  Original an der Trinity College Library; Kopie im BA. 

Text: 
Tuesday
Dear Gilbert,
I find the following remarks in the "Dictionaire Larousse": "OR n.m. (lat. aurum) métal précieux d'une couleur brillante.
Coeur d'or: excellent coeur.....C'est de l'or en barre: c'est d'une valeur certaine. Parler d'or: dire ce qu'il y a de mieux a dire..... Inattaquable...... Ce métal a été de tout temps le signe representatif de la richesse et de la puissance." ‒ Which is exactly what I mean.
I shall arrive in London either on Saturday 15th or Sunday 16th. I'll wire the exact date & time as soon as I know it, which will be in a few days. Please meet me at the station if you can.
Yours most bloodily
Ludwig
P.S. My address is
XVII Neuwaldeggerstr. 38 & never was "Prinz Eugen Str. 68" but 18 (which is different). Still, your letter, by a kind of miracle, reached me.
